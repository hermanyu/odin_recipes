Pan Fried Pork Chops Recipe

Recipe found here

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless pork chops, 3/4-inch thick.
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil

Directions

  1. Do not pat pork chops dry, so seasoning and flour will stick
  2. Season both sides of pork chops with salt and pepper, then sprinkle with flour on both sides. Use the back of a spoon to distribute the flour over the entire surface.
  3. Heat oil in a 12-inch non-stick skillet over medium heat until it simmers. Carefully add pork chops and pan fry until brown (about 4 mins). Do not disturb the pork chops until ready to turn.
  4. When you see the edges of the chops turn brown, turn the chops over, and pan fry the other side until they are no longer pink on the inside and brown on the outside (about another 4 mins). An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read 145 degrees Farenheit. Reduce heat if they are browning too quickly. Serve warm
